title: The Source in You
description: "You are not your brain. You are a formula, a complete expression of the whole, and the whole lives inside you."

---

There is something you probably already know, but that never gets the space to land.

You are not your brain. You are a formula, a complete expression of the whole, and the whole lives inside you the way a seed carries everything the tree will become. The difference between you and the Source is not nature, it is scale. Not a difference in kind, only in degree. Every wave contains the ocean. Every frequency contains the whole spectrum. You are not less than the Source. You are the Source, in one particular form.

This is not spirituality in the airy sense. It is a remembering of something that was never gone.

The world teaches you something different. The world teaches you that you are a biological machine, that consciousness is a byproduct of matter, that your decisions are the result of causal chains lying outside of you. That is the mechanical view, and it is incomplete. It sees the screen and misses the current. It describes the surface and calls it the whole story.

But there is something underneath that view.

You can notice it in the moment before a thought finishes. In the gap between stimulus and response. In the quiet when the noise falls away. There is something there that is not mechanical. Something that watches, that wonders, that chooses in ways that feel different from chemistry alone. You do not have to call it divine to notice that it is there. You only have to pay attention to your own experience and notice what is actually happening.

Consciousness may be fundamental rather than derived. Not because this is proven, but because the alternative, that consciousness arose from unconscious matter, has never been explained in a way that actually accounts for what consciousness feels like. The mechanical view describes the brain. It does not explain experience. And when a description fails to account for the thing it is describing, the description is incomplete.

This is not an argument against science. It is an argument for better science, science that takes experience seriously as a real phenomenon, not as an illusion to be reduced.

The brain is the screen. The Source is the current. You can look at the screen and think you understand electricity because you see the picture, and still know nothing about what is actually flowing through the wire.

The Void in Fractalism is where you find this. Not as emptiness, not as nihilism, but as the space where the noise becomes quiet enough to hear what is underneath. There, in that silence, you notice something that is not determined by the surface. Not free in the way theology describes it, separate from everything, making choices from nowhere. But free in another sense: free from the assumption that the surface is all there is.

There is a shadow to this as well. Not everything in the depths is light. The psyche has dark currents, instinctual pull, forces that move toward destruction as well as toward growth. Jung would say you cannot speak of the Source without acknowledging what pushes in the other direction. The Void does not promise you will find only what is pleasant there. It promises only that what is there can be seen. And what is seen can be related to rather than merely obeyed.

Determinism tells you that you are the surface. That you are your chemistry, your conditioning, your history. That you cannot be more than what caused you. This is a view designed to make you small, even if the people who hold it do not intend that. They are explaining what they see. But what they see is incomplete.

If you are the Source in your particular form, then the surface is not all you are. The patterns are things you carry, not things you are. And from that relationship, carrying rather than being identical to, something else becomes possible. Not omnipotence. Not escape from causality. But the possibility of a different relationship to what moves through you.

The question is not whether you are special in the way theology means it. The question is whether you are willing to take seriously the possibility that you are not only what your circumstances made you. Whether you can sit with that and see what follows.

What remains for me after all this: a realization that I am not only my body, not only my thoughts, not only the pain and the fears and the patterns. What is left when those things are seen clearly, not denied, not removed, but seen, is something that was here before them and will be here after. Not separate from the body. Not indifferent to it. But not exhausted by it either.

That is the Source. Not distant. Not separate. Here, in this, in you.
