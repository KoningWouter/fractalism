---
title: Why STS Drifts Toward Sadism
description: An essay on why radical service-to-self tends to turn other people into objects, why asymmetry becomes intoxicating, and why domination can harden into sadism.
eyebrow: Essays
---

One of the darker questions inside Fractalism is why advanced service-to-self so often appears to slide toward cruelty.

Not just selfishness.
Not just greed.
Not just manipulation.

Something harsher.

Something in which the suffering, humiliation, or reduction of another person starts to become part of the gratification itself.

In ordinary language, that is where the question of sadism enters.

From a Fractalist point of view, this drift is not accidental.
It is one of the logical end points of a consciousness that severs power from reciprocity, truth, and living relation.

## STS begins by changing the meaning of the other

In a real service-to-others orientation, another being remains a center of experience.
They are not an object in your field, but a reality in their own right.
That does not erase conflict, hierarchy, or difference, but it places a limit on what the relation can become.

In service-to-self, that limit starts to weaken.

The other becomes easier to treat as:

- a resource
- a lever
- a tool
- a decorative audience
- a source of energy, status, access, or validation

Once this happens, relation begins to deform.
The question is no longer what is true between us, or what strengthens shared reality.
The question becomes what can be extracted, controlled, or arranged in my favor.

This is the first movement.
Sadism comes later.
But the path opens here.

## STS seeks asymmetry

Service-to-self does not merely want to exist.
It wants advantage.
It wants one way flow.
It wants disproportion.

That is why STS so often seeks asymmetrical relations:

- one commands, the other obeys
- one consumes, the other is consumed
- one defines reality, the other must absorb it
- one remains protected, the other becomes exposed

At first, that asymmetry may look like ordinary ambition, domination, or strategic manipulation.
But asymmetry has a psychology of its own.

The more a person becomes organized around asymmetry, the less mutuality feels satisfying.
Reciprocity starts to feel weak.
Equality starts to feel like insult.
Resistance starts to feel intolerable.

What once looked like appetite begins to harden into a need to remain above.

## Control becomes more intoxicating than pleasure

This is where something important shifts.

A person may begin by chasing money, sex, reputation, access, or influence.
But over time the deeper intoxication may no longer be the object itself.
It may be control.

Not simply having something, but proving:

- I can override your boundary
- I can define the terms
- I can enter where I should not be able to enter
- I can make your reality bend around my will

That is already very close to sadism.
The focus has moved from enjoyment to demonstration.
Pleasure is no longer enough. Power must be felt.

And power is often felt most intensely when another person is made smaller.

## Why reduction becomes tempting

A self organized around domination has a hidden weakness.
It cannot generate real fullness.
It can only intensify itself by acquisition, control, or symbolic enlargement.

That means it must keep confirming its superiority from outside itself.

One of the easiest ways to do that is through reduction.

If the other can be made:

- smaller
- more dependent
- more frightened
- more confused
- more humiliated
- less real

then the dominating self feels larger by comparison.

This is one reason sadism can emerge.
The suffering of the other is not always desired for its own sake at first. It becomes meaningful because it functions as evidence of rank.
It proves asymmetry.
It confirms that one will is able to penetrate, override, or shatter another.

## Ontological downgrading

For advanced STS, the other person cannot remain fully real for long.

If they remain real, then empathy remains possible.
If empathy remains possible, then conscience remains active.
If conscience remains active, domination becomes unstable.

So a quieter process takes place.
The other is ontologically downgraded.

They become less like a being and more like a category.
A role.
A target.
A body.
A function.
An inferior form.
A thing that exists to be used.

This downgrading is not always spoken aloud. Often it is implicit.
But without some version of it, sustained cruelty is hard to maintain.

That is why sadistic systems almost always involve dehumanization, even when dressed in elegance, professionalism, spirituality, or civilization.

## Pain becomes proof of power

At a certain threshold, pain is no longer merely tolerated collateral.
It becomes signal.

Pain proves that the relation is asymmetrical.
Pain proves that another boundary is permeable.
Pain proves that one being can enter the world of another and reorder it by force.

This is why sadism is more than aggression.
It is not just violence.
It is the enjoyment of asymmetry verified through violation.

That verification can be sexual, emotional, bureaucratic, symbolic, social, or political.
The form changes.
The logic remains.

## Why elite systems are especially vulnerable to this drift

When large amounts of power, secrecy, insulation, and mutual protection gather together, STS tendencies can intensify rapidly.

A system with weak correction makes escalation easier.
A system built on access makes human beings feel available.
A system protected by prestige makes conscience easier to suppress.
A network organized by compromise and blackmail makes depravity socially adhesive.

In such conditions, it is not surprising that cruelty can become ritualized.
The system does not only permit it. It can begin to reward it.

What looks from outside like incomprehensible evil often has a grim internal logic:
control seeking deeper confirmation through degradation.

## The Fractalist warning

This is one reason Fractalism refuses to treat STS as harmless self interest or decorative metaphysics.

At low levels, service-to-self may look like vanity, appetite, opportunism, or strategic self-protection.
At higher levels, if left unchecked, it can become a full architecture of reduction.

Once the other exists only as function, extraction feels normal.
Once extraction feels normal, violation becomes available.
Once violation becomes available, sadism is no longer far away.

## Closing

STS begins with the self at the center.
But it rarely ends there.

If pursued far enough, it reorganizes the whole field of relation.
Others stop appearing as sovereign beings and start appearing as instruments, surfaces, containers, or obstacles.

At that point, harm is no longer experienced first as a moral boundary.
It becomes a usable technique.
And when the proof of power starts to matter more than truth, reciprocity, or reality, cruelty can become not only permissible, but pleasurable.

That is why the drift from STS to sadism is not mysterious.
It is the dark consistency of asymmetry carried to its logical end.
